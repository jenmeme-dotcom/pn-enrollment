PRACTICAL NURSING PROGRAM ENROLLMENT AGREEMENT

Broward-Miami Health Institute

Effective Date: February 2, 2026

1. Program Description

This Practical Nursing (PN) Program is designed to prepare students to become Licensed Practical Nurses (LPNs) in accordance with Florida Department of Education and Workforce standards. The program includes classroom instruction, laboratory work, and clinical experience.

2. Program Duration and Schedule

The program is approximately 12 months in duration. Day schedule: Monday and Wednesday, 9:00 AM - 2:00 PM. Evening schedule: Wednesday and Thursday, 5:00 PM - 9:00 PM. Students are required to maintain a minimum attendance rate of 90% to remain in good standing. Class days, times, clinical placements, and calendar dates are subject to change.

3. Tuition and Fees

The total program cost is $15,150 (subject to change). This includes tuition, books, materials, uniforms, and examination fees. Additional fees may apply for background checks and certifications.

Tuition: $14,000
Books/Supplies: $1,000.00
Registration Fee: $150

4. Payment Terms

Payment plans are available as detailed in the Payment Plan section. Full payment is required before completion of the program. Failure to maintain payment arrangements may result in dismissal.

5. Cancellation and Refund Policy

Should the student be terminated or cancel for any reason, all refunds will be made per the following refund schedule:

  1. Cancellation must be made in person or by certified mail.
  2. All monies will be refunded if the school does not accept the applicant or if the student cancels within three (3) business days after signing the Enrollment Agreement and making initial payment.
  3. Cancellation after the third business day, but before the first class, will result in a refund of all monies paid with the exception of the registration fee (not to exceed $150.00) and background fee.
  4. A student canceling after attendance has begun through 40% completion of the program will result in a Pro-Rata refund computed based on the numbers of hours completed to the total program hours. There is no refund of the registration fee and background fee after the third business day.
  5. Cancellation after completing more than 40% of the program will result in no refund.
  6. The termination date for refund computation purposes is the last date of actual attendance by the student unless earlier written notice is received.
  7. Refunds will be made within 30 days of termination or receipt of Cancellation Notice.
  8. A student can be dismissed, at the discretion of the President, for insufficient progress, nonpayment of costs, or failure to comply with the rules.
  9. If the school terminates a program for any reason prior to student graduation, the student will receive a 100% refund on monies paid to the school.
  10. For a student who is on a leave of absence, the termination date is the last date of attendance before the student left on the leave of absence.

6. Code of Conduct

Students agree to abide by all school policies and maintain professional and ethical standards. Violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including expulsion.

7. Health and Safety

Students must comply with all health and safety requirements, including immunizations, TB testing, and drug screening. Students with contagious illnesses must not attend classes.

8. Confidentiality

All patient information encountered during clinical experience must be kept strictly confidential in accordance with HIPAA regulations.

9. Grounds for Termination

A student's enrollment can be terminated at the discretion of the institution for insufficient academic progress, non-payment of academic costs, or failure to comply with rules and policies established by the institution as outlined in the catalog and this agreement.

10. Employment Assistance

Although placement assistance may be offered, the institution does not guarantee employment. Students are assisted with placement and furnished names and addresses of employment possibilities. Inquiries made to the school from potential employers will be posted on a placement opportunity bulletin board.

Placement services are provided free of charge. Nevertheless, the school does not guarantee employment.

11. Acknowledgement

This document and the catalog constitute a binding contract between the institution and the student and no further modification or representation except as herein expressed by both parties will be recognized.

12. Graduation Requirements

To graduate from Broward-Miami Health Institute, and to receive a diploma, the student must:

Complete each required course / meet each course's minimum standards in addition to obtaining an overall CGPA of a 3.0 or higher and satisfy all financial obligations.

13. Credential Awarded

Upon satisfactory completion of the program, the student will be awarded a Diploma.

Ready to Take the NCLEX? Learn How to Register

Registering for the NCLEX is a multi-step process through your nursing regulatory body (NRB) and Pearson VUE.

  1. Apply for Licensure and Registration: Submit your application to the NRB.
  2. Register with Pearson: Complete NCLEX registration and scheduling.
  3. Pay Fees: Pay exam fees and any applicable change fees.
  4. Receive ATT: Use your Authorization to Test email to schedule your exam appointment.

For official information, visit NCLEX.com.
